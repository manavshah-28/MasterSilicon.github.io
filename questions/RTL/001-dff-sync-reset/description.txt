A D flip-flop (DFF) captures the value of its input `d` on the rising edge of the clock and holds it until the next rising edge. It is the fundamental storage element in all synchronous digital design.

## Synchronous vs Asynchronous Reset

**Synchronous reset:** The reset only takes effect on the active clock edge. If `rst=1` on a rising edge, `q` goes to 0 on that edge. Reset is just another condition evaluated at the clock edge.

**Asynchronous reset:** The reset takes effect immediately, regardless of the clock. Modeled with `negedge rst_n` in the sensitivity list.

> TIP: Always use `always_ff` for flip-flops in SystemVerilog. It enforces that only non-blocking assignments (`<=`) are used and helps synthesis tools identify sequential logic.

## Key Rule: Non-Blocking Assignments

Inside `always_ff`, always use `<=` (non-blocking). This models the behavior where all right-hand sides are evaluated before any assignments take effect — simulating real flip-flop behavior where inputs are captured simultaneously.
